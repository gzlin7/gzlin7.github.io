Fog on the Charles

My sophomore year dorm room in McCormick Hall had an amazing view of the Charles River. No matter how stressful things got at school, the tranquil view from my window never failed to make everything a little brighter. When I came home for winter break, I painted the nighttime view (bottom image).

I was pleased with how the water and sky turned out, but felt I could do better with the composition and colors. At the end of the following semester, I redrew the Boston skyline as viewed from ground level rather than eighth-floor penthouse, this time paying special attention to colors and composition. I drew inspiration from the mesmerizing skyline paintings of one of my favorite artists, Ronald Kuang (@seerlight), whose palettes and work are exquisite. Equally exquisite is the work of Wenqing Yan (@yuumeiart), another of my favorite artists. I used Yuumei's digital cloud brushes in both of these paintings.
