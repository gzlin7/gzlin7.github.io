Dormspam: “Techxodus”

From January to March 2020, I drew a biweekly comic strip, Dormspam, published in The Tech. Much like the short-lived Spring 2020 semester, what started out as lighthearted doodles became depressing sketches on the state of campus. The final comic, “Techxodus,” was drawn days after evacuating MIT due to COVID-19. Dormspam subsequently went on hiatus.

The title Dormspam comes from an MIT colloquialism** for sending an email to the mailing lists of all dorms. Sometimes satire and often times club event ads, dormspam (and the flame wars it can incite) is uniquely MIT, much like the niche humor I aimed for in the Dormspam comic, which made fun of topics such as Brass Rat, XFair, Purell dispensers, and tetazoo glounge.

One rule for sending dormspam is that the sender must close the message by indicating the color of his or her underwear. Or any color, really. I've tried to pay homage to this tradition in the artist's signatures of Dormspam comics. I hope to continue Dormspam sometime in the future, when I'm feeling more inspired.

**To my knowledge, “dormspam” is both a verb and a noun.
