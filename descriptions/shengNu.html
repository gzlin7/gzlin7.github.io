剩女 (shèng nǚ)

Based on the Chinese concepts of 剩女 (“leftover” women) and 姻缘红线 (Red Thread of Fate), this piece challenges the idea that a woman’s value lies in marriage. Usually tied around the pinky, the Red Thread supposedly connects soulmates, i.e. marriage partners, yet the subject’s Red Thread is severed to indicate that marriage is choice, not destiny. The thread entangling the subject represents the confines placed on women by societal and cultural expectations to be obedient caretakers and homemakers and nothing else. Having been labeled a 剩女, the subject stares into the eyes of the observer, unfazed.

I have nothing against marriage and was simply intrigued (and disturbed) by the 剩女 concept. I decided to paint this on a whim while procrastinating. Additionally, I had been wanting to explore using Red Thread and Chinese calligraphy as themes in a piece, and everything sort of converged into this. It is not a self-portrait.
